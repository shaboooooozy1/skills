# Triage Playbook

## Fast Inbox Pass (5-minute)

1. Group emails by sender type: leadership, customer, internal team, automated.
2. Flag messages with dates, approvals, incidents, or blockers.
3. Assign priority `P1-P4`.
4. Draft responses for `P1` and `P2` first.
5. Add every explicit commitment to an action tracker.

## Reply Skeleton

Use this structure for most professional replies:

1. Acknowledge context.
2. State decision or requested action.
3. Provide deadline and owner.
4. Close with next checkpoint.

Example:

- "Thanks for raising this. We will ship the fix by April 21, 2026. Jordan owns implementation; Priya will validate QA by April 22, 2026. I will send final confirmation after deployment."

## Escalation Heuristics

Escalate when any applies:

- external customer impact
- policy, legal, security, or compliance exposure
- conflicting direction from multiple senior stakeholders
- no owner and deadline inside 48 hours

## Calendar + Email Coordination

When a meeting is needed, include:

- purpose in one line
- required attendees only
- pre-read links
- explicit decision expected by end of meeting
